My Story

Built on Three Decades of Bold Decisions

I began my career in 1997 at Arthur Andersen in Madras, where I discovered a passion for complex, high-stakes problem-solving — helping global companies navigate India's regulatory environment, facilitating over $800M in foreign investment approvals, and restructuring contracts for some of the world's largest energy and technology firms.

That foundation in consulting led me to the United States, where I joined TRW Automotive in Tennessee before earning a Master of Accounting and Financial Management from Keller Graduate School of Management in Chicago. Then came KPMG in San Francisco — four years of leading risk advisory engagements for companies ranging from $600M medical device manufacturers to a $60B internet giant, sharpening my ability to see systemic risk and opportunity inside complex organisations.

"The cosmos is not a hostile wilderness. It is our home — and understanding it requires both the telescope and the examined life." — Vinay Lakshman

At Thoratec Corporation, a MedTech pioneer making ventricular assist devices that keep patients alive while they await heart transplants, I progressed from Risk Management Manager to Operations Controller — leading 5-year strategic planning, overseeing two manufacturing sites in California and Zurich, managing $60M in global inventory, and reporting directly to the Audit Committee of the Board.

The next chapter brought me home to India, where Rane Brake Lining — a publicly listed automotive components company — asked me to lead the business as CEO & Managing Director. Over five years, I grew revenue from €44M to €56M, doubled profitability, drove R&D investment from 2.5% to 8.5% of sales, shifted the renewable energy mix from 18% to 40%, and led the company to win the Deming Grand Prize from JUSE, Japan — one of the world's most rigorous quality certifications. The company was also recognised as a Great Place to Work.

The Philosophical Pause

Between 2020 and 2022, I made a deliberate choice that surprised many colleagues: I stepped away from the executive world to study Indian Philosophy of Non-Duality at Arsha Vidya Gurukulam. For two years, I immersed myself in Vedantic logic, ethics, and the deepest questions of consciousness and existence.

This wasn't a retreat from leadership — it was an intensification of it. The study of Non-Duality trains the mind to perceive the interconnectedness beneath apparent differences, to reason with rigour and act with equanimity. It changed the quality of my thinking in ways that no MBA or boardroom ever could.

"Science without religion is lame, and religion without science is blind." — Albert Einstein

The Space Chapter

I am now pursuing the Master of Space Studies (MSS) at the International Space University in Strasbourg, graduating in July 2026. My specialisation covers space entrepreneurship, economics, commercial space strategy, policy and law — the intersection where my 20+ years of business experience becomes directly applicable to the fastest-growing frontier in human history.

My individual research project — Impact of Philosophy and Theology on Space Exploration in the 21st Century — argues that humanity's long-term success in space demands not just scientific and engineering excellence, but a coherent philosophical and ethical framework. My team project contributes to a global communication strategy for deep space exploration.

I am seeking full-time opportunities in the commercial space sector starting July 2026 — in strategy, business development, finance, operations, or policy roles where I can help organisations scale responsibly and think clearly about the long arc of human spaceflight.
