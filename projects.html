ISU Individual Research · January 2026

Impact of Philosophy & Theology on Space Exploration in the 21st Century

This interdisciplinary paper examines the convergence of philosophy, theology, and science within the framework of 21st-century space exploration. The 17th-century Scientific Revolution created a "Great Schism" between empirical science and metaphysical inquiry — a division this research argues we must now overcome.

Tracing philosophical contributions from Konstantin Tsiolkovsky and Carl Sagan through to contemporary space ethics, and examining theological perspectives from Christianity, Buddhism, and Hinduism's cyclical cosmology, the paper makes the case that humanity's enduring success beyond Earth depends on integrating scientific rigour with philosophical wisdom.

Philosophy of Science Space Ethics Tsiolkovsky · Sagan Cosmology Overview Effect Non-Duality Buddhism Hinduism Christianity

Key finding: Space exploration is both a pursuit of "cosmic truth" and a technology that serves human needs — requiring a collaborative framework in which science clarifies the what, while philosophy and theology provide the why and the ethical compass for humanity's journey beyond Earth.

📍 International Space University, Strasbourg
